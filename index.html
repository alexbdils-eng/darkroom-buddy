📖 Chemistry Guide

🧪 Developer

Converts exposed silver halide into metallic silver, creating the visible image. Time and temperature are critical — overdevelopment increases contrast and grain.

🛑 Stop Bath

Acidic solution that instantly halts development. Without it, developer carries over causing uneven results. 30 seconds with gentle agitation is enough.

🔧 Fixer (Hypo)

Removes unexposed silver halide, making the image permanent. Under-fixing leaves a milky/purple cast. Film needs ~5 min; don't over-fix paper.

💧 Hypo Clear

Washing aid that cuts final wash from 30 min to 5-10 min. Saves water and time. Use between fixing and final wash.

🫧 Photo-Flo

Wetting agent preventing water spots on film. One drop per tank. Do NOT agitate — let it sit perfectly still.

🌡 Temperature & Time

Standard: 68°F/20°C. Each 1°F above reduces time ~10%. Below 65°F chemistry becomes sluggish.

🔄 Agitation

Inversion: Flip 4x in 5s, every 30-60s.
Rotary: Continuous in Jobo, reduce times ~15%.
Tray: Rock corner-to-corner for prints.

⬆ Push / Pull

Push (+): +1 stop ≈ +30% time. More grain & contrast.
Pull (−): −1 stop ≈ −20% time. Less contrast.
Best within ±2 stops.

⚠ Top 5 Mistakes

1. Wrong temperature
2. Inconsistent agitation
3. Under-fixing (purple negs)
4. Loading film in light
5. Skipping Photo-Flo
