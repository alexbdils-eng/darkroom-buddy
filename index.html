📖 Chemistry Reference

🧪 Developer

Converts exposed silver halide into metallic silver. Time and temperature are critical — overdevelopment increases contrast and grain.

🛑 Stop Bath

Acidic solution that instantly halts development. 30 seconds with gentle agitation is enough.

🔧 Fixer

Removes unexposed silver halide, making the image permanent. Under-fixing leaves a milky/purple cast. Film needs ~5 min.

💧 Hypo Clear

Washing aid that cuts final wash from 30 min to 5-10 min. Saves water and time.

🫧 Photo-Flo

Wetting agent preventing water spots on film. One drop per tank. Do NOT agitate — let it sit perfectly still.

🌡 Temperature & Time

Standard: 68°F/20°C. Each 1°F above reduces time ~10%. Below 65°F chemistry becomes sluggish.

🔄 Agitation

Inversion: Flip 4x in 5s, every 30-60s. Rotary: Continuous, reduce times ~15%. Tray: Rock corner-to-corner for prints.

⬆ Push / Pull

Push (+): +1 stop ≈ +30% time. More grain & contrast. Pull (−): −1 stop ≈ −20% time. Less contrast. Best within ±2 stops.
